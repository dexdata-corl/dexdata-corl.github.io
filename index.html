Overview

The recent progress in imitation learning (IL), data-driven reinforcement learning (RL), and foundation models has sparked growing interest in building general-purpose robotic agents capable of performing diverse, complex tasks in real-world environments. Achieving this goal—especially for bimanual and dextrous systems—requires large-scale, high-quality datasets that capture a wide spectrum of behaviors, sensing modalities, and environmental diversity. This workshop will explore strategies for scalable data collection, including teleoperation via leader-follower setups, VR interfaces, shared autonomy, simulation-generated trajectories, and cross-domain dataset aggregation. We will also address challenges in data augmentation, evaluation metrics, benchmarking protocols, and the infrastructure required to collect, curate, and serve data at scale. By bringing together the robot learning community across IL, RL, and foundation model approaches, we aim to foster the exchange of ideas, highlight promising tools and techniques, and discuss the key open challenges in scaling data-driven robotics across complex, yet meaningful tasks.
