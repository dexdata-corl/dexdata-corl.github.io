Overview

Scaling both robot data and model capacity has shown the potential for foundation models to enable general-purpose robotic agents capable of performing diverse and complex tasks in real-world environments. However, realizing this potential—particularly for bimanual and dexterous manipulation—requires large-scale datasets that capture a broad spectrum of behaviors, sensory modalities, and environmental variability. Collecting such data remains a major bottleneck due to challenges in human supervision, embodiment mismatch, and hardware limitations.

This workshop will examine scalable data collection strategies, including teleoperation via leader-follower control, virtual reality interfaces, shared autonomy, simulation-generated trajectories, and cross-domain dataset aggregation. It will also address challenges in leveraging large-scale dexterous data, such as data augmentation, evaluation metrics, standardized benchmarks, and the infrastructure needed to collect, curate, and serve data efficiently.

By convening researchers across imitation learning, reinforcement learning, and foundation model approaches, the workshop aims to identify key tools, methods, and open problems in scaling data-driven robotics for complex manipulation tasks.

Discussion Questions by Topic:

Human-in-the-Loop Demonstrations and Shared Autonomy
  • What are the most scalable approaches to collecting bimanual and dexterous demonstrations, including passive (e.g., video, wearable sensors) and active (e.g., VR, kinesthetic) methods?
  • Can shared autonomy or corrective feedback loops reduce reliance on fully teleoperated data?
Multimodal Data Collection and Representation
  • How can we design datasets that capture richer, multimodal observations at scale?
  • What are the best practices for annotating and representing such data for generalizability?
Simulation vs. Real-World Data Generation
  • When do sim2real strategies (e.g., domain randomization, real-to-sim pretraining) work well, and where do they fall short?
  • Can simulation help bootstrap real-world data collection via curriculum learning or synthetic scaffolds?
Scaling with Foundation Models
  • How can LLMs/VLMs assist in specifying tasks, labeling, or filtering large-scale robotic datasets?
  • Can generative models synthesize useful robot data (e.g., goal states, trajectory sequences or reward signals)?
Benchmarking, Infrastructure, and Open Access
  • What are principled ways to evaluate dexterous manipulation across tasks and domains?
  • How can we build standardized, scalable infrastructure (e.g., RLDS, TFDS) to support collaborative, multi-lab dataset creation?
  • What are the best practices for sharing and maintaining open datasets and tooling to promote reproducibility and community adoption?
